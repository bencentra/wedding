FAQ

Is there a COVID-19 vaccine requirement?

We are requiring that all guests who are able to have received a COVID-19 vaccine. In addition, we would prefer that guests are also up to date on their booster shots. Finally, please comply with the masking rules of any of our venues. For more information, visit the Massachusetts COVID-19 website.

Will the party be indoors or outdoors?

Our main event at Lord Hobo will be a mix of indoor and outdoor spaces. The outdoor patio connects to one of the beer halls via open garage doors. We've rented the whole brewery for our private event. The afterparty at Hong Kong is indoors in a private room. Our Saturday Hangout will be in our backyard if the weather is nice. Monday Breakfast at the hotel will be indoors as well, in a private function room.

Can I bring a +1?

Of course, significant others are welcome! If you didn't already include them when you RSVPd, just let us know.

Can I bring my children?

Yes! Children are also welcome, though unfortunately they won't be able to enjoy any beer. We'll have some games on the patio, as well as a quiet corner of the brewery for them to relax in.

I'm gonna take lots of photos! How do I share them with you?

The best way to share your photos with us is to add them to this Google Drive folder. You can also share them on social media with the hashtag #dicentra2022 so that we can find them later. We won't have a professional photographer at the party, so please take lots of photos!

Is there a dress code for any of the events?

Nope! Please do wear clothes though. Note that if you want to participate in a brewery tour, you'll need closed-toed shoes.

I don't actually like beer. Will there be other drinks?

Of course! Lord Hobo also serves soft drinks, wine, and a guest hard cider. There will be no spirits or cocktails at Lord Hobo. To get an idea for what will be on tap, check out the taproom website. There will be cocktails at the afterparty!

How do you recommend getting around town from the hotel?

On the 14th, check out the bus schedule for getting to and from the party. Note that there will no bus back from the afterparty; to get back to the hotel, we recommend taking an Uber or Lyft. To get downtown, the hotel offers a shuttle to the Anderson/Woburn Commuter Rail station, which will take you into Boston's North Station. If you drive into Boston, you can easily park at Alewife Station and take the Red Line, or park at the Boston Common Garage in the heart of downtown.

I live in the city. Do I have to drive to Lord Hobo?

No you don't! The bus will pick you up from Alewife before the party and bring you back to Harvard after - for the afterparty, or to take the public transit home. Check out the bus schedule for more details. If you do drive, Lord Hobo offers free parking in their lot or on the adjoining street.

I want to explore the Boston area. What should I do while I'm here?

We've had many good times and made many great memories here in Boston. Below is a list of some of our favorite places and activities, as well as some of the classics.

TapClick for Boston activities!

If you like beer, there are many other great craft breweries: Aeronaut, Lamplighter, Remnant, Idle Hands, Notch, Night Shift, Trillium, and many more. And of course there's the big ones: Harpoon and Sam Adams.

There are also some local distilleries: Short Path, Bully Boy, and Grand Ten. Plus some world-class cocktail bars: Drink, Backbar, and The Baldwin Bar, which is right in Woburn and attached to an amazing Chinese restaurant, Sichuan Garden.

There are lots of other great restaurants! Some of our favorites include Gustazo (cuban), Colette (french patisserie), Martsa on Elm (tibetan), Sweet Cheeks (bbq), Limoncello (italian), Yume Wo Katare (ramen), Dali (tapas), Gene's (chinese), Bronwyn (german), Nijiya (sushi), and surprisingly, the Boston Public Library (afternoon tea)!

If you want to explore Boston and its history, you can walk the Freedom Trail. Following the bricks will lead you past historic sites, monuments, famous graveyards, the U.S.S. Constitution ("Old Ironsides"), and most importantly, amazing pastry shops in the North End. I'm partial to the cannolis at Mike's.

Summer is a great time to experience Boston! Take a stroll through the Common or Public Garden. Walk along the Esplanade or the Greenway, or go kayaking on the Charles. Visit Chinatown for East Asian food or the North End for Italian (and the occasional festival). Check out the Aquarium, Museum of Fine Arts, Institute of Contemporary Art, or the Museum of Science (great for kids). Harvard also has excellent Art and Natural History museums!

But wait, there's more! If you like sportsball, the Red Sox will be playing the Yankees at Fenway on August 13th. If you want to gamble, there's the new Encore Casino. If you like nature, go for a hike in the Middlesex Fells or Blue Hills Reservation, or go for a walk around Fresh Pond or Walden Pond.
